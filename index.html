City Gardens

Allotments in Dublin

Learn more about City Gardens

City Gardens is an initiative created in conjunction with local communities and Dublin City Council, the aim of this initiative is to encourage people who live in the inner city, particularly in apartment and flat complexes to spend some time outside gardening and socialising using public lands divided into allotments which can be used by members of the public.

Why have an allotment?

An allotment can provide:

  • A good source of affordable food you know the origins of.
  • You can grow your own fresh fruit, vegetables and flowers.
  • Working an allotment is a healthy physical recreation for all age groups.
  • Social outlet – People from all age groups and a wide variety of social backgrounds use the allotments.
  • All the people can learn from each other through gardening together.
  • Allotments have an educational value – a number of schools and educational groups have allotments.

How to apply for an allotment?

Interested in getting an allotment? Just fill in the contact form. You will be placed on a waiting list for your preferred site and as soon as an allotment becomes available you will be notified.
